As per my evaluation, Nmap receives the highest rating (ie., of 26 / 30). Nmap, short for Network Mapper, was first published in 1997 as an article in Phrack magazine. It helps in identifying hosts and services on the network and listen to the open ports, TCP/IP fingerprinting, version detection, etc. It comes as a package with many Linux distros, and is very easy to install. The commands are very simple to execute and the outputs are easy to understand. It is free of cost, hence no licensing fees. It has evolved over the years and is extremely flexible. If used properly, it helps in protecting network from hackers and can listen to the hosts without them knowing. Regarding the reputation of NMap, one can see the list of movies it has been used in. From Matrix to Bourne series, directors and story writers love NMap and often it is found in the backdrop of any hacking scene[1]
